About Me

Hello! My name is Rodrigo Zapata. Im a spanish programming graduate based in Newcastle-upon-tyne. I have recently completed my BSc Course in Computer Science and Games design in Northumbria University. I have achieved first class honours and have worked on mutliple projects throughout my stay in university. I have done multiple videogames, from its code to its sound design, participated in a game jam, created a game controller designed for the disabled, designed or contributed to multiple website projects and have even worked with tools like MATLAB with machine learning. I aim to create projects that improve someone's day, and allow wider accessibility for everyone. Click the buttons below to access my CV and other documents.

My hobbies include competitive online videogames and cooking. I particularly enjoy cooking and I'm currently looking at joining a cooking course to expand on my skills. I regularly cook healthy (or unhealthy, depends on what I want that day) meals and like to innovate, and iterate on my experiments from my day prior. My work in the kitchen often translates well to my work on the screen. I have done volunteer work for a group of nuns, feeding the local pool and homeless of my area as well as being an english teacher to kids in less fortunate areas of Madrid. I have done 2 years of teacher assistance during my IB studies. Throughout my life i have learned teamwork, compassion, patience, and creativity.

Shoot 'em Up

Utilizing a custom shell provided by an instructor i was able to produce a short "shoot 'em up" style game done within visual studios, the shell is only capable of drawing shapes on the screen, the handling of game objects, from creation to destruction, from message boradcast and reception, from objectives to game feel and even sound effects and controls were all made by me in C++.

Racing Game in UE4

A simple car racing game designed by me within unreal engine 4, the idea was to utilise research into formula 1 vehicle physics as well as research on similar games, to ultimately craft a racing game with bespoke physics, while some of the ideas were unable to be finished due to time most of the project is available and theres a short youtube demo showcasing a lap being ran in the engine. To watch the video just press on the image.

WebGL Animation

An animation project done in WebGL and javascript. All the models are created by hand and the textures were sourced from free sites. The code uses WebGL to implement realtime lighting, normalmaps, as well as simple animation. The entire project is ran through browsers. Python and Web

A collaborative group effort of 5 people. We worked with a private client to provide them with a web app according to their specifications. Part of the project required that we keep regular communication with out client and to ensure that they were happy with the result. Changes were done regularly and uploaded to a private github repo. I was in charge of the blog page. By the end, users were able to create blogs through an admin account, which were sent to a server, and posted on the main page. It also had blog editing, and some basic custom CSS to ensure everything fit with the aesthetics of the website.
